# Isekai Hero Goal

## North Star

The Isekai Hero should feel like a genre-parody power-fantasy character who
enters the Spire with outsider logic. They become powerful by understanding
systems faster than the Spire expects, turning that advantage into dramatic
spikes of strength.

This goal should guide card, relic, potion, and mechanic work while Slay the
Spire 2 is still evolving. It defines the character promise and design
boundaries, not a final mechanic set or card list.

## Character Pillars

### Jobs

Jobs answer the question, "What class did I get in this world?"

- Jobs are the clearest isekai identity hook.
- Jobs should create different build directions that can coexist in one card
  pool.
- A Job should feel like a role or ruleset the hero adopts, not just a renamed
  buff package.

### Exploits

Exploits answer the question, "How do I break this world's systems?"

- Exploits should reward sequencing, context, loopholes, or unusual card use.
- This pillar covers modern knowledge, game literacy, appraisal screens,
  crafting logic, science tricks, status windows, and comedy built on rules that
  should not work here.
- Exploits should make the player feel clever rather than merely stronger.

### Cheat Skills

Cheat Skills answer the question, "When does the protagonist moment happen?"

- Cheat Skills should support huge finishers, rule-bending bursts, and snowball
  moments.
- Their payoffs should be earned through drafting, setup, choices, or risk.
- They may be dramatic and comedic, but they should not become the whole class
  identity.

## Spire Fit

The Isekai Hero should fit Slay the Spire 2 by supporting:

- Clear card families and build incentives.
- Strong run-to-run draft decisions.
- Payoffs that emerge from deck construction and combat sequencing.
- A few memorable flavor-first cards outside the major pillars.

Individual cards may overlap with official class space, but the character
should not define itself primarily through existing class identities:

- Ironclad-style Strength, Exhaust, or self-damage.
- Silent-style discard autoplay, Shivs, or Poison.
- Regent-style alternative energy, Forge-style single-card buildup, or generic
  card generation.
- Necrobinder-style summon health storage, Soul draw loops, Doom execution, or
  giant attacks alone.
- Defect-style Orbs, zero-cost spam, or Powers as the main engine.

## Design Filters

Use these questions when proposing future mechanics and cards:

1. Does this feel like an isekai trope instead of a normal fantasy hero card?
2. Does it support Jobs, Exploits, Cheat Skills, or a strong flavor exception?
3. Does the player feel smart or powerful for using it?
4. Would the same mechanic make more sense on an existing Slay the Spire 2
   class?
5. Is the power fantasy earned through choices, risk, setup, or deckbuilding?

Mechanics are especially promising when they connect pillars:

- A Job changes how an Exploit card is valued.
- An Exploit accelerates a Cheat Skill.
- A Cheat Skill changes shape based on the chosen Job.

## Acceptance Checks

The goal is working when future card sets can produce runs that feel like:

1. **Job Run:** I got a class and built around what it lets me do.
2. **Exploit Run:** I abused strange interactions and solved fights through
   knowledge.
3. **Cheat Run:** I assembled absurd protagonist moments without every turn
   being absurd.
4. **Hybrid Run:** My Job gave structure, my Exploits created openings, and my
   Cheat Skills closed fights.

The goal is drifting when:

- The strongest concept is only generic overpowered anime attacks.
- Jobs are flavor labels with no build meaning.
- Exploits are novelty cards with no play pattern.
- The class copies an official archetype and swaps in isekai names.

## Tone And Next Step

The tone should lean into genre parody: stat screens, Truck-kun jokes, Jobs,
knowledge exploits, and dramatic protagonist nonsense are welcome. Specific
reference cards can exist, but the overall character should be broader than any
single isekai series.

## First Card Plan

This first pass uses Job Powers as a working assumption. A card that says "if
you have a Job" checks whether a Job Power is active. The plan assumes multiple
Jobs may coexist until the Job mechanic is designed in detail.

"Conditional effect" is design shorthand for the extra rule text on cards such
as "if the enemy intends to attack" or "if this kills." If cards like `I Am
Atomic` and `Protagonist Privilege` survive implementation planning, conditional
effects should get an explicit implementation hook instead of relying on rules
text parsing.

Cards that already exist as placeholders in code should move toward the planned
roles below before the pool expands around them. This includes `Status
Appraisal`, `Truck-kun`, `Return by Death`, `Megiddo`, and `I Am Atomic`.

Parenthetical values show planned upgrades, such as "Gain 5 (8) Block." An
`Upgraded:` note marks a behavior change that is not captured by a number swap.

### Attacks

- [ ] **Tutorial Sword** - Common Attack, 1 Energy. Deal 7 (9) damage. If
  you have a Job, deal 4 (6) more damage.
- [ ] **Boss Telegraph** - Common Attack, 1 Energy. Deal 6 (8) damage. If
  the enemy intends to attack, gain 5 Block. Upgraded: when the conditional
  effect triggers, draw 1 card.
- [ ] **Last-Hit Bonus** - Common Attack, 1 Energy. Deal 8 (11) damage. If
  this kills, draw 1 (2) cards and gain 1 Energy.
- [ ] **Training Arc** - Common Attack, 1 Energy. Deal 5 damage twice. If
  you played a Skill this turn, gain 4 Block. Upgraded: when the conditional
  effect triggers, deal damage one additional time.
- [ ] **Truck-kun** - Uncommon Attack, 2 Energy. Deal 15 (19) damage to ALL
  enemies. If this kills an enemy, gain 1 Energy. Upgraded: also draw 1 card.
  Exhaust.
- [ ] **Cross-Class Combo** - Uncommon Attack, 1 Energy. Deal 6 damage once
  for each different card type you played before this card this turn.
  Upgraded: count this Attack too.
- [ ] **Monster Grinding** - Uncommon Attack, 1 Energy. Deal 10 (13) damage.
  If this kills, permanently increase this card's damage by 3 (4).
- [ ] **Megiddo** - Rare Attack, 2 Energy. Deal 24 (30) damage. If you
  played a Power this combat, deal 12 (16) damage to ALL enemies.
- [ ] **I Am Atomic** - Rare Attack, 3 Energy. Deal 36 (45) damage to ALL
  enemies. Costs 1 less this combat whenever you trigger a conditional effect.
  Upgraded: the first conditional effect you trigger each turn reduces this
  card's cost by 2 instead. Exhaust.

### Skills

- [x] **Status Appraisal** - Common Skill, 0 Energy. Look at the top 3 (5)
  cards of your draw pile. Put one into your hand and discard the others.
- [ ] **Item Box** - Common Skill, 1 Energy. Gain 7 (10) Block. Choose a
  card in your hand. Retain it. Upgraded: choose up to 2 cards instead.
- [x] **Seen it Coming** - Common Skill, 1 Energy. Gain 6 (8)
  Block. If the enemy intends to attack, apply 1 (2) Weak.
- [ ] **Route Guide** - Common Skill, 1 Energy. Gain 5 (7) Block. Look at
  the top 4 cards of your draw pile. Put one on top and the rest on the bottom.
  Upgraded: put up to 2 on top in any order.
- [ ] **Job Change** - Uncommon Skill, 1 Energy. Choose a Job card from
  your draw pile or discard pile and put it into your hand. It costs 0 this
  turn. Upgraded: it costs 0 for the rest of combat. Exhaust.
- [ ] **Dodge the Bad End** - Uncommon Skill, 1 Energy. Choose a card in
  your discard pile. Put it on top of your draw pile. It costs 0 next turn.
  Upgraded: put it into your hand instead and it costs 0 this turn.
- [ ] **Applied Physics** - Uncommon Skill, 1 Energy. Gain 8 (11) Block.
  The next Attack you play this turn ignores Block. Upgraded: draw 1 card.
- [ ] **Return by Death** - Rare Skill, 2 (1) Energy. At the start of your
  next turn, return to your HP, Block, and status values from when this was
  played. Lose all Energy next turn. Exhaust.
- [ ] **System Menu** - Rare Skill, 2 Energy. Choose a card in your hand.
  For the rest of combat, its conditional effects are always active. Upgraded:
  you may choose a card in your discard pile instead. Exhaust.

### Powers

- [ ] **Job: Appraiser** - Uncommon Power, 1 Energy. At the start of your
  turn, look at the top 3 cards of your draw pile and put them back in any
  order. Upgraded: you may put one of them on the bottom.
- [ ] **Job: Spellblade** - Uncommon Power, 1 Energy. Once each turn,
  after you play a Skill, your next Attack this turn costs 1 less and deals 4
  (7) more damage.
- [ ] **Job: Alchemist** - Uncommon Power, 1 Energy. The first time each
  turn you apply a debuff, gain 4 (6) Block and your next Attack against that
  enemy deals 4 (6) more damage.
- [ ] **Grinding Montage** - Rare Power, 2 Energy. At the start of your
  turn, upgrade 1 (2) random Attack or Skill in your hand for this combat. If a
  chosen card is already upgraded, reduce its cost by 1 this turn.
- [ ] **Protagonist Privilege** - Rare Power, 2 Energy. Once (twice) each
  turn, when a conditional effect on one of your cards triggers, repeat that
  effect.

### Follow-Up Design Work

Before these cards become implementation tasks, decide:

1. Whether Jobs are truly Power cards, a separate status, or a broader combat
   choice.
2. Which conditional effects are allowed to be repeated or forced active.
3. Whether run-scaling cards such as `Monster Grinding` fit the mod's desired
   balance and save-data complexity.
